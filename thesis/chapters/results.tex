\paragraph{Objective.} 

\textit{TractoInferno} is the world's largest open-source, multi-site tractography database aimed at machine learning tractography approaches and related ML algorithms. The entire dataset provides 284  samples acquired from 3T scanners across 6 different sites. The available data includes T1-weighted anatomical images, single-shell diffusion MRI (dMRI) acquisitions, and reference streamlines for 30 delineated bundles generated using 4 tractography algorithms~\cite{poulin2022tractoinferno}. From this dataset, 167 subject acquisitions from the training subset were used to validate the spatial agreement between pyramidal tract (PYT) reference bundles from \textit{TractoInferno} and the CST bundles extracted by \texttt{csttool}. This validation represents how well an automated, deterministic, atlas-based extraction pipeline recovers a known tract across a large, heterogeneous cohort.

\paragraph{Validation Contract.}

A formal validation contract governs the comparison process. Each subject-hemisphere pair is assigned exactly one outcome status. INVALID\_SPACE cases are excluded from quantitative analysis and reported separately. DEGRADED cases remain in the analysis but are flagged. This separation ensures that infrastructure failures do not contaminate algorithmic performance assessment.

\begin{table}[h]
	\centering
	\caption{}
	\begin{tabular}{lll}
		\hline
		Status & Condition & Metrics \\
		\hline
		OK & Spatial checks pass, candidate non-empty, no structural warnings & Computed \\
		DEGRADED & Spatial checks pass, but candidate empty or below minimum streamline count & Computed (may be zero) \\
		INVALID\_SPACE & Spatial compatibility violated (affine mismatch, grid incompatibility, or candidate missing) & Not computed \\
		\hline
	\end{tabular}
\end{table}

In total, 334 hemispheres were compared (167 subjects $\times$ 2). Out of these, 330 yielded valid metrics i.e. status OK. No DEGRADED cases were observed. Two subjects (numbers 1025 and 1074) had no PYT bundles, resulting in an INVALID\_SPACE tag for 4 hemisphere comparisons.

\paragraph{Spatial Compatibility.}

The validator also verifies that candidate and reference bundles share a compatible coordinate system. Three requirements must be fulfilled for the check to pass. First, the candidate tractogram from \texttt{csttool} must be mappable into the reference \textit{TractoInferno} FA space. Second, affine matrices must agree within a tolerance of 0.5 mm (RMS). Third, voxel grid dimensions and orientation must match the reference anatomy. If any checks fail, the comparison is recorded as INVALID\_SPACE.

The spatial overlap between two binary volumes is measured using the Dice coefficient (Eq.\ref{eq:dice_coefficient}).

\begin{equation}
	\mathrm{Dice} = \frac{2 \times |\mathrm{A} \cap \mathrm{B}|}{|\mathrm{A}| + |\mathrm{B}|}
	\label{eq:dice_coefficient}
\end{equation}
