\section{TractoInferno Validation}
\label{sec:results-tractoinferno}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Dataset and Setup}
\label{subsec:tractoinferno-setup}

\paragraph{Objective.} 

\textit{TractoInferno} is the world's largest open-source, multi-site tractography database aimed at machine learning tractography approaches and related ML algorithms. The entire dataset provides 284  samples acquired from 3T scanners across 6 different sites. The available data includes T1-weighted anatomical images, single-shell diffusion MRI (dMRI) acquisitions, and reference streamlines for 30 delineated bundles generated using 4 tractography algorithms~\cite{poulin2022tractoinferno}. From this dataset, 167 subject acquisitions from the training subset were used to validate the spatial agreement between pyramidal tract (PYT) reference bundles from \textit{TractoInferno} and the CST bundles extracted by \texttt{csttool}. This validation represents how well an automated, deterministic, atlas-based extraction pipeline recovers a known tract across a large, heterogeneous cohort.

\paragraph{Validation Contract.}

A formal validation contract governs the comparison process. Each subject-hemisphere pair is assigned exactly one outcome status. INVALID\_SPACE cases are excluded from quantitative analysis and reported separately. DEGRADED cases remain in the analysis but are flagged. This separation ensures that infrastructure failures do not contaminate algorithmic performance assessment.

\begin{table}[h]
	\centering
	\caption{}
	\begin{tabular}{lll}
		\hline
		Status & Condition & Metrics \\
		\hline
		OK & Spatial checks pass, candidate non-empty, no structural warnings & Computed \\
		DEGRADED & Spatial checks pass, but candidate empty or below minimum streamline count & Computed (may be zero) \\
		INVALID\_SPACE & Spatial compatibility violated (affine mismatch, grid incompatibility, or candidate missing) & Not computed \\
		\hline
	\end{tabular}
\end{table}

In total, 334 hemispheres were compared (167 subjects $\times$ 2). Out of these, 330 yielded valid metrics i.e. status OK. No DEGRADED cases were observed. Two subjects (numbers 1025 and 1074) had no PYT bundles, resulting in an INVALID\_SPACE tag for 4 hemisphere comparisons.

\paragraph{Spatial Compatibility.}

The validator also verifies that candidate and reference bundles share a compatible coordinate system. Three requirements must be fulfilled for the check to pass. First, the candidate tractogram from \texttt{csttool} must be mappable into the reference \textit{TractoInferno} FA space. Second, affine matrices must agree within a tolerance of 0.5 mm (RMS). Third, voxel grid dimensions and orientation must match the reference anatomy. If any checks fail, the comparison is recorded as INVALID\_SPACE.

\subsection{Spatial Overlap}
\label{subsec:tractoinferno-dice}

The spatial overlap between two binary volumes is measured using the Dice coefficient (Eq.\ref{eq:dice_coefficient}).

\begin{equation}
	\mathrm{Dice} = \frac{2 \times |\mathrm{A} \cap \mathrm{B}|}{|\mathrm{A}| + |\mathrm{B}|}
	\label{eq:dice_coefficient}
\end{equation}

Across all 330 valid hemisphere comparisons, the mean Dice coefficient was $0.283 \pm 0.065$ (median 0.285, range 0.053–0.471). A consistent hemispheric difference was observed: right CST yielded higher overlap ($0.299 \pm 0.060$) than left CST ($0.268 \pm 0.066$). Figure~\ref{fig:dice-distribution} shows the full distribution; Figure~\ref{fig:dice-by-hemisphere} shows the per-hemisphere breakdown.

\subsection{Precision and Recall}
\label{subsec:tractoinferno-precision-recall}

Precision (Eq.~\ref{eq:precision}) measures the proportion of the candidate bundle that overlaps the reference, while recall (Eq.~\ref{eq:recall}) measures the proportion of the reference recovered by the candidate.

\begin{equation}
	\mathrm{Precision} = \frac{|\mathrm{A} \cap \mathrm{B}|}{|\mathrm{A}|}
	\label{eq:precision}
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
	\mathrm{Recall} = \frac{|\mathrm{A} \cap \mathrm{B}|}{|\mathrm{B}|}
	\label{eq:recall}
\end{equation}

Mean precision across all 330 valid comparisons was $0.854 \pm 0.066$, while mean recall was $0.172 \pm 0.047$. The pronounced asymmetry — high precision, low recall — directly reflects the streamline count mismatch between deterministic and probabilistic tractography described in the following subsection. Figure~\ref{fig:precision-recall} shows per-subject precision and recall values split by hemisphere.

             330 valid comparisons.}

\end{figure}

\subsection{Streamline Counts}
\label{subsec:tractoinferno-streamlines}

\texttt{csttool} produced a mean of 4,093 streamlines per bundle (range 358–9,676). The TractoInferno reference bundles contained a mean of 61,188 streamlines (range 19,244–169,699), approximately 15 times more than the \texttt{csttool} candidate. This ratio is a direct and expected consequence of the tracking methodology: the reference bundles were generated using probabilistic algorithms that explore a distribution of fibre orientations per voxel, whereas \texttt{csttool} uses deterministic single-peak tracking and therefore produces compact, high-precision bundles. The count mismatch is the primary driver of the low recall observed in Section~\ref{subsec:tractoinferno-precision-recall} and is discussed further in Section~\ref{sec:discussion-dice}.

\section{Determinism Validation}
\label{sec:results-determinism}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Setup}
\label{subsec:determinism-setup}

To evaluate the reproducibility of the pipeline, each test subject was processed three times using identical parameters: random seed 42, single-threaded execution (\texttt{OMP\_NUM\_THREADS=1}), and \texttt{csttool} v0.4.1 (git commit \texttt{1433ac4}). Preprocessing was performed once per subject; all three repeated runs consumed the same fixed preprocessed NIfTI volume as input, isolating tracking, extraction, and metric computation. Outputs were compared bitwise using SHA-256 checksums applied to tractogram files, scalar maps (FA, MD, RD, AD), per-tract metrics CSVs, and PDF reports.

\subsection{Single-Subject Test}
\label{subsec:determinism-single}

Subject sub-1204 (3T scanner, 32 gradient directions, 1\,mm isotropic voxels) was used as the primary single-subject test case. The whole-brain tractogram contained 575,111 streamlines. CST extraction yielded 3,243 left-hemisphere streamlines and 4,038 right-hemisphere streamlines. Scalar maps were computed on a $134 \times 178 \times 136$ voxel grid. Across all three repeated runs, every output was bitwise identical: tractograms, FA/MD/RD/AD maps, per-tract metrics CSVs, and PDF reports all produced the same SHA-256 checksum. Figure~\ref{fig:subject-paired} illustrates the overlap of key metrics across the three runs.

\begin{figure}[ht]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figs/results/subject_paired.png}
    \caption{Paired CST metrics (FA mean, streamline count) across three repeated runs
             for sub-1204. Overlapping points confirm bitwise identical outputs.}
    \label{fig:subject-paired}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Multi-Subject Test}
\label{subsec:determinism-multi}

To confirm that determinism is a general property of the pipeline rather than an artefact of a single acquisition, five subjects from the TractoInferno trainset were tested: sub-1007, sub-1030, sub-1165, sub-1204, and sub-1276. Each was processed three times under the conditions described in Section~\ref{subsec:determinism-setup}, yielding 15 total pipeline executions. All five subjects received a verdict of \textsc{bitwise\_identical}: every tractogram, scalar map, metrics CSV, and PDF report matched SHA-256 checksums across all three runs.
